# Astarion Leveling Guide - Gloom Stalker Assassin (Ranged)

**Final Build:** Ranger 5 / Rogue 4 (Assassin) / Fighter 2 / War Cleric 1
**Party Priority:** #3 (friend-controlled, ranged)
**Core Weapon:** Titanstring Bow (entire game once acquired)
**Starting Stats:** STR 8, DEX 17 (15+2), CON 14, INT 8, WIS 16 (15+1), CHA 10

---

## Level-by-Level Progression

### Level 1: Ranger 1
- **Stats:** STR 8, DEX 17, CON 14, INT 8, WIS 16, CHA 10
- **Skills:** Stealth, Sleight of Hand, Perception, Survival, Athletics
- **Favoured Enemy:** Bounty Hunter (Ensnaring Strike, proficiency in Investigation)
- **Natural Explorer:** Urban Tracker (proficiency in Sleight of Hand)
- Use a shortbow or light crossbow until Titanstring Bow is acquired

### Level 2: Ranger 2
- **Fighting Style:** Archery (+2 to ranged attack rolls)
- **Spells:** Hunter's Mark, Ensnaring Strike
- Archery offsets Sharpshooter penalty later; always pick this

### Level 3: Ranger 3 (Gloom Stalker)
- **Subclass:** Gloom Stalker Conclave
- **Dread Ambusher:** +3 to initiative, bonus attack on first turn of combat (extra 1d8 damage)
- **Umbral Shroud:** Become invisible in darkness (free stealth in dim/dark areas)
- **Spell:** Longstrider (lasts until long rest, no concentration)
- Build identity online: first-turn burst specialist

### Level 4: Ranger 4
- **Feat:** Sharpshooter (-5 attack / +10 damage, ignore cover, no long range penalty)
- Only toggle the -5/+10 when you have advantage or high hit chance

### Level 5: Ranger 5
- **Extra Attack!** Two attacks per Attack action
- **Spell:** Spike Growth (great area denial)
- **Replace:** Hunter's Mark -> Pass Without Trace (+10 party Stealth)
- First turn now: 2 attacks + Dread Ambusher bonus = 3 attacks

### Level 6: Rogue 1
- **Sneak Attack:** 1d6 extra damage (once per turn, need advantage or ally adjacent)
- **Expertise:** Sleight of Hand, Stealth
- Astarion is now the party's primary pickpocket

### Level 7: Rogue 2
- **Cunning Action:** Hide, Dash, or Disengage as bonus action
- Bonus action Hide after attacking = regain advantage for next turn

### Level 8: Rogue 3 (Assassin)
- **Assassinate: Initiative** -- advantage on attack rolls against creatures that haven't taken a turn yet
- **Assassinate: Ambush** -- auto-critical hit on surprised creatures
- **Assassin's Alacrity** -- restore action + bonus action at start of combat if you surprised enemies
- This is where surprise rounds become devastating

### Level 9: Rogue 4
- **Feat:** Ability Improvement +2 DEX (DEX 17 -> 19)
- No Hag's Hair (goes to Shadowheart); DEX stays odd until Mirror of Loss

### Level 10: Fighter 1
- **Second Wind:** Heal 1d10 + Fighter level as bonus action
- **Fighting Style:** Defence (+1 AC)
- Stacks with Archery from Ranger; two fighting styles active

### Level 11: Fighter 2
- **Action Surge!** One additional action per short rest
- Build goes nuclear: surprise round = 5-6 auto-crit attacks with Sharpshooter

### Level 12: War Cleric 1
- **War Priest:** Bonus action weapon attacks (WIS mod charges per long rest = 3)
- **Cantrips:** Guidance, Thaumaturgy
- **Prepared Spell:** Sanctuary (protect yourself or ally after burst turn)
- More bonus action attacks on top of everything else

## Surprise Round Burst Sequence

This is the reason you play Assassin. Set it up properly and most fights end in one turn.

1. **Setup:** Approach solo, invisible (Umbral Shroud in darkness or Potion of Invisibility)
2. **Initiate:** Attack from stealth to trigger Surprise
3. **All hits are auto-crits** (Assassinate: Ambush) with **advantage** (Assassinate: Initiative)
4. **First action:** Attack (2 hits from Extra Attack) + Dread Ambusher bonus attack = **3 attacks**
5. **Bonus action:** War Priest attack (another auto-crit) OR Cunning Action: Hide
6. **Action Surge:** 2 more attacks (still auto-crit if target is surprised)
7. **Total: 5-6 attacks, ALL critical hits, each with Sharpshooter +10 damage**
8. **After burst:** Umbral Shroud to go invisible again, or continue fighting normally

### Damage math (Level 12, Cloud Giant Strength)
Per auto-crit Sharpshooter hit: 2d8 (bow crit) + 5 (DEX) + 8 (STR via Titanstring) + 10 (Sharpshooter) + misc = ~31-35 damage per arrow
With 5-6 arrows: **~155-210 damage in one turn**, plus Sneak Attack (applied once).

## Key Mechanics

**Titanstring Bow:** Adds STR modifier to damage on top of normal DEX-based attacks. With Cloud Giant Strength (27 STR), that is +8 damage per hit for free.

**Sharpshooter:** -5 to attack, +10 damage. Offset with Archery (+2), high ground (+1d4), Oil of Accuracy (+2), advantage from stealth/Assassinate. On surprise rounds the -5 barely matters since you have advantage + auto-crit.

**Assassinate:** On surprise rounds, all attacks against surprised creatures are automatic critical hits. Crits double ALL dice (weapon, Sneak Attack, Dread Ambusher, etc.). This is the build's identity.

**Pickpocketing:** Expertise in Sleight of Hand + high DEX makes Astarion the party's primary pickpocket. Steal from merchants freely.

**War Priest:** Gives WIS modifier (3) bonus action attacks per long rest. These are full weapon attacks, not off-hand. They benefit from all damage bonuses.

## Combat Tips

**Setting up Surprise:** Separate Astarion from the party. Use Pass Without Trace (+10 Stealth) before approaching. Enter stealth, get into position, attack. The rest of the party can join after.

**When Surprise fails:** You still have a strong kit. Extra Attack + Dread Ambusher + Sharpshooter is good damage. Use Cunning Action: Hide to regain advantage for next turn.

**Target priority:** Eliminate spellcasters and high-threat enemies first. The burst damage is wasted on low-HP fodder.

**Positioning:** Always seek high ground for the +1d4 attack bonus. Stay at range. Use Cunning Action: Dash or Disengage if enemies close in.

**Resource pacing:** Action Surge recharges on short rest. Use it aggressively in hard fights, then short rest. War Priest charges are long rest only -- save them for important encounters.
